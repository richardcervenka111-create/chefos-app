The Problem

Every kitchen has accepted the same chaos.

The head chef's technique lives in her head, and nowhere else.

A note gets passed at the pass, and half of it never reaches the line.

Prep gets forgotten until the first ticket's already up.

A tray goes in the bin, and nobody can say what it actually cost.

Something goes wrong — and the question becomes who, never why.

Every kitchen has learned to live with this. Sautero hasn't.

The Vision

A kitchen that remembers.

Software changed how companies keep the truth — one record everyone trusts, instead of a dozen private ones. Kitchens never got that.

Sautero treats a kitchen the same way. What one cook knows, every cook can know. What's happening on the line right now is visible to whoever needs to see it — not just whoever's shouting loudest.

Nothing depends on being remembered, because nothing has to be.

Chaos isn't a kitchen tradition. It's just the oldest bug nobody's fixed.
